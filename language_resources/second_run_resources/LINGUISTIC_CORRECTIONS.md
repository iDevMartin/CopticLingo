## UNIT 3: ARTICLES & GRAMMAR BASICS

### **Article System Issues**

The original curriculum had inconsistencies with article forms. Here's the correct Bohairic system:

#### **Definite Articles (based on Mallon & Morphology document):**

**Masculine Singular:**
- **Standard form:** Ⲡⲓ- (pi-)
  - Example: ⲡⲓⲣⲱⲙⲓ (the man)
- **Before certain consonants (β, λ, μ, ν, ρ):** Ⲡ-/Ⲫ- changes
  - Example: ⲫⲓⲱⲧ (the father) - Ⲡ becomes Ⲫ before vowel
- **Phonological rule:** Π → Φ before voiced consonants or stressed vowels

**Feminine Singular:**
- **Standard form:** Ϯ- (ti-)
  - Example: ϯϩⲓⲙⲓ (the woman)
  - Example: ϯⲙⲁⲩ (the mother)

**Plural (both genders):**
- **Standard form:** Ⲛⲓ- (ni-)
  - Example: ⲛⲓⲣⲱⲙⲓ (the men)
  - Example: ⲛⲓϩⲓⲙⲓ (the women)

**Indefinite Article:**
- **All genders/numbers:** ⲟⲩ- (ou-)
  - Example: ⲟⲩⲣⲱⲙⲓ (a man)
  - Example: ⲟⲩϩⲓⲙⲓ (a woman)

**CORRECTION IN UNIT 3:**
- Original had inconsistent use of Ⲡ/Ⲫ/Ⲡⲓ forms
- Corrected to follow phonological rules from morphology document

The original curriculum mixed Sahidic and Bohairic forms. Key differences:

| Feature | **Sahidic** | **Bohairic** |
|---------|------------|--------------|

| Example: "sister" | ⲥⲱⲛⲉ | ⲥⲱⲛⲓ |

## PRONUNCIATION CORRECTIONS

### **Letter Ⲓ (Iota/Yota) Rules:**
From morphology document:
- **At word start:** Usually consonantal "y" (e.g., Ⲓⲏⲥⲟⲩⲥ = Yesous)
- **Between consonants:** Vocalic "ee" (e.g., ⲣⲱⲙⲓ = rōmee)
- **After vowels:** May form diphthong

### **Letter Ⲅ (Gamma) Rules:**
- **Before e, i, y, u:** Hard "g" (e.g., ⲅⲉⲛⲟⲥ = genos)
- **Elsewhere:** Soft "gh" (e.g., ⲁⲅⲁⲡⲏ = aghapi)

### **Phonological Rule (Π, Τ, Κ → Φ, Θ, Χ):**
From morphology page 1:
- Π, Τ, Κ become Φ, Θ, Χ before:
  1. Voiced consonants (β, λ, μ, ν, ρ)
  2. Stressed vowels (unless word-initial)
- Example: ⲡⲓⲱⲧ → ⲫⲓⲱⲧ ("the father" - Π→Φ before vowel)
